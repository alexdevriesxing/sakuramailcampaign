## Account eligibility and security

Users must be legally able to contract, provide accurate information and protect their account access. They are responsible for activity in their workspace.

## Acceptable use

Sakura Mail may be used only for lawful messages to recipients the customer is permitted to contact. Customers must keep evidence of consent or another lawful basis and provide accurate sender identification, required disclosures and a valid postal address.

## Prohibited use

Prohibited conduct includes purchased, harvested or scraped lists; unsolicited bulk mail; misleading sender information or subjects; phishing; malware; credential theft; illegal offers; harassment; suppression evasion; security probing; and activity that damages deliverability or provider reputation.

## Customer responsibility

Customers control recipient selection and message content and remain responsible for CAN-SPAM, CASL, GDPR/ePrivacy and other applicable requirements. Product controls assist compliance but do not create permission to send.

## Credits and payment

Credits are prepaid at the price displayed at checkout. One credit is reserved for each eligible selected recipient. An attempt may count after acceptance into the delivery pipeline even if it later bounces. Credits have no cash value and are non-transferable. Taxes, refunds, disputes and expiration rules must be finalized before production.

## Service limits

Delivery, inbox placement, timing and uninterrupted availability are not guaranteed. Sending may be throttled, paused or rejected based on quotas, reputation, content, provider limits, security or law.

## Customer data

Customers retain rights in their data and grant Sakura Mail a limited license to host, format, transmit and process it only as needed to provide, secure and support the service and comply with law.

## Suspension and termination

Accounts may be suspended for abuse, security threats, nonpayment, legal risk or provider-policy violations. Customers may close their account and request deletion subject to billing, suppression, security and legal retention.

## Warranty, liability and indemnity

These clauses must be tailored by qualified counsel. The final agreement should include appropriate service disclaimers, liability limits, exclusions, indemnities and mandatory-law carve-outs.
